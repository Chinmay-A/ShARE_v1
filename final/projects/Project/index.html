Projects

ShARE students acquire top corporate skills through training, projects with start-ups, and real-life projects, they learn to work across borders, appreciate and embrace cultural differences. Most importantly, students collaborate on solving major societal issues and on cases to transform companies into “Do Well Do Good".

ShARE students apply their problem-solving skills to use by working on a multitude of problem statements focused on a diverse set of verticals ranging from Technology, Sustainable Energy, and Mobility to Global Economy and City & Consumer.

Startup Projects

ShARE students help early-stage start-ups and NGOs tackle problems faced by them and provide the best possible solution using their extensive training. It is a collaborative experience for both the students and startups.

Learning Unit Projects




ShARE IIT Bombay stood 2nd in the Asia Case Study Challenge organized by ShARE. 15 teams from Asia competed for the best learning unit or group presentations of the ShARE Leadership programme and the top 3 slide decks were presented to a ShARE project lead.

The City and Consumer learning unit comprised Anuj Agrawal, Sunandinee Mehra, Kartik Laddha, and Shreyasi Mandal, and worked on the topic "How will sustainable and inclusive cities in your country look like in 2030?". Learning Unit Manager Rohith Karthikeyan and Senior Member Bhavya Jain spearheaded the project.
